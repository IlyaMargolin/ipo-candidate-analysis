# Methodology

## Objective

The goal is to create a transparent framework for identifying and comparing companies that may become IPO candidates.

## Recommended score dimensions

### 1. Growth
Measures whether the company shows a scale trajectory compatible with public-market interest.

Possible signals:
- revenue growth;
- customer growth;
- geographic expansion;
- product expansion.

### 2. Quality
Measures whether growth is supported by healthy economics.

Possible signals:
- gross margin;
- recurring revenue quality;
- retention quality;
- operating leverage.

### 3. Market
Measures whether the category is large and strategically attractive.

Possible signals:
- total addressable market;
- category maturity;
- competitive intensity;
- investor appetite for the sector.

### 4. IPO readiness
Measures whether the company can plausibly transition into a public-company environment.

Possible signals:
- governance;
- reporting discipline;
- management depth;
- compliance preparedness.

### 5. Risk
Captures the factors that weaken or delay IPO candidacy.

Possible signals:
- regulatory risk;
- capital intensity;
- concentration risk;
- path-to-profitability risk.

## Scoring design principles

- Keep the model interpretable.
- Separate hard data from analyst judgment.
- Make weights explicit.
- Avoid presenting the final score as certainty.

## Interpretation

A high score means the company looks stronger within the chosen framework.  
It does not guarantee a successful IPO, good valuation, or favorable listing conditions.
